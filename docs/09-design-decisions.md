# Design decisions log

Decisions from prototype playtests (ported into the webview client [src/client/](../src/client/)). When there's a dispute over "how it should work" - look here first.

| # | Decision | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Board - "single board" (suspects x categories, candidate chips in cells) instead of 3 tabs | The deduction chain jumps between categories on almost every step - tabs break the context; you can't see the whole state at once |
| 2 | "Cross-out" model: click = cross out a candidate | Playtest: the player naturally thinks in exclusions ("it's not him") rather than assignments |
| 3 | Check counts the single remaining candidate as the answer | Playtest 1: the player crossed everything out correctly and got "0/12, not enough deductions" - perceived it as a bug |
| 4 | Full auto-cascade (auto-checkmark cells + columns) - **rejected** | Playtest 2: the board "lives its own life", the player loses the cause-and-effect link |
| 6 | Live clue status (🟢 satisfied / 🔴 violated / gray) | Player's suggestion. Reinforces clarity and gives instant feedback about a contradiction; computed from the player's marks, not from the solution - no spoilers |
| 7 | Drag-and-drop of values into cells - **deferred** | Deduction in this genre is mostly "NOT" statements, drag can't express them (you can't "drag NOT-pizza"); conflicts with scrolling on mobile and in the Reddit webview. Return at the polish stage as an extra gesture for positive assignments |
| 8 | "Active clue" (panel + Prev/Next + selection mode) - **removed** | Playtest 3: the concept is unclear and confusing. The clue list with live statuses fully replaces it |
| 9 | All automation removed - the board is fully manual | Playtest 3: "less automation was clearer", "let nothing happen automatically". Even optional toggles and local auto-cross-out are removed. `yes` no longer crosses out the row/column - only by hand. Crossing out dims candidates, so the remaining answer is visible without indicators; `effectiveValue` (read-only) keeps "crossed everything out = solved" |
| 10 | Board highlight/outline on hovering a clue - **removed** | Playtest 3: confusing. Clues and the board are visually independent; the player keeps the link in their head (that's what deduction is). A hint marks only its own clue in the list (in yellow), it doesn't touch the board |
| 11 | Unified "post" metaphor: everyone posted exactly once at their own time. All clues are about "posted" ("was online / had business / found / touched" removed) | Audit: 5 different verbs for one relation threw people off on first read. "Was not online at 15:00" read ambiguously (not at that moment / left before 15:00 - interval vs instant). "Posted" is an instant event: both ordering ("earlier") and exclusion ("not at 15:00") are unambiguous |
| 12 | Official solution chain switched to a path **without "proof by contradiction"** | The former step "John's item" was solved by trial with a contradiction. The order "first Blue=15:00 ⇒ spoon@15:00, then clue 9 places scroll@09/keyboard@12" makes the whole puzzle derivable by direct elimination - every move is certain without hypotheses |
| 13 | Clue 8 (Paul ≠ Purple) redundancy confirmed | Brute-force: the solution is unique without it too; the other 9 clues are load-bearing. Keep it as an optional crutch or drop it for the minimal set |
| 14 | `yes` confirmation removed from the board - pure toggle (click = cross out/restore, two states) | Playtest 4: the green confirmation badge collapsed the cell and hid the other versions, but "when you're solving, it's important to see". Since "crossed everything out = solved" (dec. 3), a separate "confirm" step is redundant: one version left = the player chose it. Clicking it just crosses back out. We don't add an answer indicator (dec. 9): a non-crossed chip is already bright against the dimmed ones. `yes`/right-click remain only in the tabbed view (UI comparison) |
| 15 | Design tokens instead of ad-hoc values: an elevation ladder of surfaces, 3 text steps, 8 font steps, 4pt spacing, 4 radii ([10-design-spec.md](10-design-spec.md)) | Audit (4 agents): 14 different font-sizes (incl. fractional), 4 shades of gray, text `#566083` with 2.8:1 contrast (WCAG fail), a mishmash of 9/10/12/16/18/20 radii. All "text-background" pairs of the new palette recomputed and pass AA |
| 16 | Flairs - Okabe-Ito palette + an R/B/G/P letter in the chip (not emoji circles) | Circles 🔴🟢 are indistinguishable under deuteranopia (dist 54 against a ~60 threshold), state was conveyed by color alone. A letter works under any CVD and in grayscale; a crossed-out chip keeps its tint (30% mix) and letter - you can see WHAT was crossed out |
| 17 | Orange - only CTA/progress/brand echo; the view switcher is a muted ⚙ panel BELOW the grid; yellow - exclusively hint | Hierarchy audit: 5 competing orange accents diluted the CTA; playtest tools stood above the grid louder than the category headings. Demotion preserves the function (a decision from the methodology: contentious variants - as toggles) while removing the noise |
| 18 | Mobile layer: sticky suspect names + edge-fade on board scroll (do NOT shrink chips below 30px); controls stick to the bottom, the CTA is full-width; result screen: primary - "Send to comments", "Close" - ghost, the final table is collapsed | Mobile audit: names ran off the edge UNREACHABLY (center+overflow), the HUD spilled over by 110px, a white scrollbar; on the result screen the brightest button was "Close", though the core of the community loop is share and voting |

| 19 | Format - **inline post in the feed** (Devvit tall, 512px): header+casebar+controls always visible, the middle scrolls inside (`@media max-height:720px`) | User's decision: "the game right in the feed". The 720 threshold also closes the "gap" at 620-720px where the CTA went below the fold |
| 20 | Result screen - **light card over the dark game**, no scrolling on desktop | User's decision by reference (r/ColorPuzzleGame): "game on dark, results on white"; "short and to the point, everything visible at once". Light tokens overridden locally in the `.result` scope, contrasts recomputed |
| 21 | Distribution histogram - **by solve time** (not by moves) | The number of moves in the cross-out model is almost the same for everyone - the distribution degenerates; time differentiates players. One series: neutral bars #6b7590, accent bar "you" #d9631e (validated: ≥3:1, CVD ΔE 67), labels selective - "you" and the peak |
| 22 | Clue 8 (Paul ≠ Purple) - kept as a crutch for beginners | User's decision; revisit during Case #1 playtests |
| 23 | Game screen = the post itself (~700×512, like feed games on Reddit): brand header, footer and legends removed; HUD (progress/timer, errors - after the first) in the case row; instructions and legend - behind a "?" button; playtest switcher - below the post | User's reference (r/ColorPuzzleGame): "minimum information on the game screen, only the game itself" |
| 24 | Phone - zero scrolling: a compact grid "suspect name above a row of cells" (fits in 390px without horizontal scroll), all 10 clues visible without scrolling | Complaint from a phone: "I don't like scrolling clues and scrolling the board". Trade-off: 24px chips (smaller than recommended tap targets, compensated by an expanded tap zone and long-press names) - verify with a playtest |
| 25 | In the feed (512px post on a phone) the middle scrolls inside the post - it's a preview; full no-scroll is in the expanded view (Devvit expanded) | 10 clues + grid physically don't fit in 390×512; expanding the post on mobile is Devvit's standard path |
| 26 | The "Check case" button and error counter - **removed**. The case closes itself: all 12 cells are deduced and match the solution → ~0.9s pause → slow appearance of the result card. After solving, in place of the hint - a "🏆 Results" button (reopen) | User's decision: "it's clear from the left anyway, when all clues are green". Errors as a concept no longer exist: a wrong full layout simply highlights the violated clues in red - fix it and continue |
| 27 | Solved suspect row animation: a one-time green flash of the cells + a permanent green frame highlight + a ✓-pop by the name | User's request: a micro-celebration for each "closed" character gives a rhythm of progress (4 small rewards before the final one) |
| 28 | Community-building loop **removed**: vote for tomorrow's case theme, submit/suggest a suspect, an in-game deduction card for sharing - deleted | The theme is cosmetic (a reskin: the logical skeleton doesn't depend on it) ⇒ voting for the theme = a facade; the answer is deterministic ⇒ shared deduction cards are the same for everyone = duplicate spam; discussion lives natively in Reddit comments. Return only if participation affects difficulty/logic, not the nouns (the theme) |
| 29 | The "Tabs" view variant and the view switcher (#viewswitch/devbar) **removed** - a single view remains: single board (rows - suspects, columns - categories, candidate chips in cells) | Board beat tabs in playtests. The engine concept "confirmed value" (state 2) was kept intentionally - as an engine hook, not as a UI element |
| 30 | Core promise/tagline updated: the right half "Shape tomorrow's mystery" / "Every solve helps build tomorrow's stranger case" removed. Product = a daily task + an argument about logic in the thread + a streak | After removing the community loop (dec. 28) the promise "you're building tomorrow's case" is unsupported. New: "Daily deduction. One clever case a day" / "Every day, one clever case. Solve it, argue your logic in the thread, keep your streak" |
| 31 | The puzzle's logic and difficulty - **100% deterministic code**, AI is not involved; AI/templates - flavor only (theme, names, items). Engine: [src/server/engine.ts](../src/server/engine.ts) | Whatever the AI produces - it still has to run through the solver (uniqueness + difficulty), and it doesn't reliably hit both targets ⇒ it's cheaper and more deterministic to generate with code. Difficulty is an emergent property of the clue set: you measure it with the solver, you don't write it. See [doc 04](04-puzzle-engine.md) |
| 32 | Difficulty = **TIER by a fork of two solver models** (WEAK: suspect×category board; GRID: all pairwise value×value grids + transitivity). 🟢 weak-forced / 🟡 grid-forced-not-weak / 🔴 not-grid-forced (a guess is needed) | "Whether you need to guess" depends on the techniques available to the player. The old "difficulty = number of clues" doesn't work. Implemented and validated on 10 cases + 20 independent solvers |
| 33 | Default daily difficulty - **🟡**: a fair path without a single guess exists, but it needs cross logic (flair↔time↔item), not reading by rows | User's decision: "I'd always make it yellow, solving was pretty good". The sweet spot: fair (no guessing) + "the brain kicks in". 🟢 - a warm-up, 🔴 - special days for the smart |
| 34 | Reclassification of the manual cases: **Case #0 = 🟡** (the difficulty reference), **Case #1 = 🟢** (too easy - requires not a single cross inference) | Measured by the engine: Case #0 weak-NOT-forced/grid-forced; Case #1 weak-forced. The "clunky" first case is closer to the target bar than the "clean" second one. Naive solvers stumbled on Case #0 precisely because it's 🟡 |
| 35 | "Transitivity depth" as a smooth scale between WEAK and GRID - **rejected** | It doesn't separate the cases (all have 2-3) and doesn't respect "easy = low depth" (Case #1 is also depth 2). The boundary is in fact binary: you run value×value grids or you don't. We keep the fork of two models, without an intermediate one |
| 36 | Hackathon layer in the prototype: full English + de-Reddit ("Coat" instead of flair, Omar instead of ModBot, no r/-mentions in visible strings; "flair" in the UI replaced with "rank"); confirm() and placeholder buttons removed | TIER 0 strategies (removing the "Russian" and "on-the-nose theming" penalties); run through an adversarial content audit: Cyrillic 0, Reddit terms 0, placeholders 0 |
| 37 | Honest numbers: when N<50 solvers - "You're the Nth detective today" instead of a percentile and histogram; the distribution and % - only when N≥50 from the same buckets (one number in the hero and on the chart). The "day 2 / at scale" playtest toggle below the post shows both states | De-slop from the strategy: fake social proof = minus trust in everything. The toggle is a demo tool, labeled as playtest |
| 38 | Onboarding = 3 coach marks over the real case (clues → chip → progress), skip in 1 tap, the timer is paused until the tutorial ends; ?tut=1/0 hooks | TIER 0 p.6: "learn it in 15 seconds", nobody reads the text behind "?" |
| 39 | **[REMOVED 2026-07 - stripped out, see dec. 41]** "Detective trail": suspects in the grid numbered ①-④, after solving - a copyable string "Case #N · time · path ②→①→④→③ · 💡n · clues #… untouched". Order = the order rows were closed; "untouched" = clues that got satisfied only on the last move; undo rolls back this bookkeeping too | TIER 1.1: the share artifact encodes the PATH, not the answer (analogous to the Wordle grid); numbering in the grid makes the string readable without a legend |
| 40 | **[2026-07: live, NOT removed - the 🔥/⚖️/🌿 vote + "envelope" = the mechanism of a fair 🟢↔🟡 ramp; see dec. 41]** Community verdict: solvers vote 🔥/⚖️/🌿 (one vote per case, "play again" doesn't reset it) + a "sealed envelope" CASE #N+1 with a "HARD leads - 62%" ticker and a countdown; an epilogue line "🥇 Yesterday: u/X · 2:41 · N detectives · voted TIER (pct%)" in the post header (on a phone - a short form); rank in the result stats | TIER 1.2-1.3: visible causality of the choice + recognition by name + a public ledger. Launch texts - docs/12-launch-content.md |
| 41 | **(2026-07) Rebranding Logic Thread → Deducto** + cleanup of the share layer. App slug `deducto-puzzle` (`devvit.json` `name`); the "Deducto" brand set in the post title, menu items ("Deducto: publish a case" / "publish showcase"), `src/shared`, `src/server`, `src/client`. **The "detective trail" (dec. 39) stripped out**: the copyable share string in the client no longer renders (in `main.ts` only a dead reference to it remains in a comment) | The rename went through all the code in one sweep. Trail dropped per dec. 39: the string encoded the path but gave little value and cluttered the result card. ## Methodology (learned from a mistake)

- Change **one variable per iteration** - changing four aspects of the interaction at once made the behavior inexplicable.
- Frame contentious levels of automation as **toggles in the prototype**, to compare them live in one session.
- Interaction acceptance criterion: the player finishes a game without once asking "why did that happen?".
